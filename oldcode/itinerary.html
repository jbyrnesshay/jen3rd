Itinerary

Bharkor St

Bharkor Square is an old section of Lhasa, which preserves a more traditional village feel, with various traditional markets, shops, and restaurants. As the site of Jokhang Temple, it is also important to pilgrams and to residents who come for religious practicies on a daily basis.

Temples, Monasteries, and Palace

Lhasa still has a number of prominent historical temples and sites. 4 of these constituted the bulk of my itinerary.

Jokhang Temple

located in the Old Town area of Lhasa, encircled by Barkhor Street.

Potala Palace

A huge palace. Now a museum.

Drepung Monastery

In my view, this was the the place with the most monastic setting, though not far at all from the other sites

Sera Monastery

Known for its "monk debates", where the monks gather in the courtyard and engage in philosophical debates which are marked by a set of rituals and formal gestures such as "hand clapping" (the monks slap their hands forecefully while making their points).
